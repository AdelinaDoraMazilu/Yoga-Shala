A yoga home, not just a studio

Yoga Shala Shangri La is a light-filled practice room with straw mats underfoot, cream walls, and a great gong watching over the space. Here, yoga is not a performance — it is a homecoming. Classes are small and personal, taught with care for where you are today: your breath, your body, your season of life.

Whether you arrive stiff from a desk, restless from a busy mind, or simply curious, you'll find a practice that meets you gently — Hatha and Vinyasa to move, Yin to soften, and sound to let go completely.

the paradise within

Small circles, real attention

Intimate groups mean the teacher actually sees you — alignment, breath, mood. Adjustments are personal, never one-size-fits-all.

Every body, every level

Absolute beginners are the shala's favourite guests. Props, patience and playful variations make each posture yours.

Sound as medicine

Gong baths and Peter Hess® singing bowls carry the nervous system into deep rest — the kind of stillness sleep sometimes forgets.

The gong & the Peter Hess® bowls

At the heart of the shala hangs a great gong. During a gong bath you simply lie down, wrapped in a blanket, while waves of overtones wash through the room — thought quiets, breath deepens, the body remembers how to rest. Alongside it live the Peter Hess® therapeutic singing bowls, each tuned to resonate with a different region of the body.

tap the gong — go on, it rings ✨

What a sound journey feels like

You arrive, you lie down, you do absolutely nothing — and that is the whole practice. The gong builds slow tides of sound; the bowls are placed on and around the body, their vibration felt as much as heard. Most people describe the hour after as “the deepest rest I've had in years.”

Sound journeys happen every week — find them marked in gold on the schedule below. No experience needed. Blankets provided. Snoring forgiven.
